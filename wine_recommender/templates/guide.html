Understanding Wine Basics

Types of Wine

Red Wine

Made from dark-colored grape varieties, fermented with the grape skins.

White Wine

Made from green or yellow grapes, typically fermented without the skins.

Rosé

Made from red grapes with limited skin contact during fermentation.

Sparkling

Contains significant levels of carbon dioxide, making it fizzy.

Wine Characteristics

Body

Light
Full

How the wine feels in your mouth - from light to full-bodied.

Tannins

Soft
Strong

Creates a dry, puckering sensation - common in red wines.

Acidity

Low
High

Sweetness

Dry
Sweet

Ranges from bone dry to very sweet - determined by residual sugar.

Wine Tasting Steps

1

Look

Observe the wine's color, opacity, and viscosity. Tilt the glass and look at the color from the rim to the center.

  • Check the clarity
  • Note the intensity
  • Observe the color variation
2

Smell

Swirl the glass to release aromas. Take short sniffs to identify primary, secondary, and tertiary aromas.

  • Identify fruit notes
  • Detect oak influence
  • Notice unique aromas
3

Taste

Take a sip and let it coat your mouth. Notice the flavors, texture, and finish.

  • Assess sweetness level
  • Note the acidity
  • Evaluate the finish

Wine Storage Tips

Temperature

Store between 45-65°F (7-18°C). Avoid temperature fluctuations.

Light

Keep away from direct sunlight and fluorescent lights.

Position

Store bottles horizontally to keep corks moist.

Humidity

Maintain 60-70% humidity to prevent cork drying.
